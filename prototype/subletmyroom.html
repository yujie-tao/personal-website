Sublet My Room

This is a semester-long project for my Software Startup class under the UNC Department of Computer Science.

Through market research and interviews, my teammates and I identified housing sublease as a pertinent problem of college students as there is a disconnection between two sides of students. Students usually refer to friends/post on social media when looking for a room, but both ways rarely guarantee a good match. To address the issue, we designed a reliable web portal for students to connect with each other during the subleasing process. By collecting preference from two sides of students, we are able to provide users with a unified platform to search and post subleasing. Users can be matched based on requirements such as price, location, roommate gender, etc.

Our primary target users are students in the RTP area. To test there is a robust market need, we conducted interviews, made a prototype and launched a landing page. On the landing page, we opened two sign up sheets to collect sublease information. From Mar.20 to Apr. 20, we reached 700+ page views on our landing page, with 80 responses for sign-up forms.

By having two sides of subleasing information, we further test whether we can connect both sides as a third-party role. By using the sublease information collected from sign up form, we started to match student renters with student subleasers manually.

Going forward, we reflected that we need to address the legal implications of serving as a third-party leasing company, automating the matching process by building a functional website similar to Airbnb and figure out ways to create profit.

During the project, I:

  • Conducted market research and interviews to understand user problem and test market need
  • Determined product functionalities, prototyped in Axure RP and iterated the demo
  • Developed a prototype in Express.js and MongoDB
